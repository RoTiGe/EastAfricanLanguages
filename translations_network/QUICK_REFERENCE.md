## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

### 1. **Start Formal, Then Adjust**
When in doubt, use formal language. You can always become more casual later.

### 2. **Greetings Are Sacred**
In East African cultures, never skip greetings. Always greet before making requests.

### 3. **Use Titles**
When formal, use titles: "sir/madam", "Mr./Ms.", professional titles.

### 4. **Match Energy**
If someone greets you casually, you can respond casually. If formal, stay formal.

### 5. **Emergency = No Formality**
In emergencies, just shout for help. Formality doesn't matter.

### 6. **Family = Affection**
With family, use terms of endearment: "love", "dear", "sweetheart".

### 7. **Time Matters**
Don't say "good morning" in the evening. Match your greeting to the time.

### 8. **Listen & Learn**
Pay attention to how native speakers adjust their language in different contexts.
